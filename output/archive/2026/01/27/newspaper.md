## The Front Page

### AI2’s Open Coding Agents: The Quiet Shift Toward Autonomous Software Maintenance
Source: https://allenai.org/blog/open-coding-agents
HN: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46783017
The Allen Institute’s AI2 released a suite of open-source coding agents capable of iterative debugging and dependency resolution—useful, but early adopters report a 30% false-positive rate in suggested fixes. The tradeoff? Reduced toil for routine tasks, at the cost of introducing subtle, machine-generated anti-patterns into mature codebases.

### ShapedQL: A SQL Engine That Bends Ranking to Its Will—At What Cost?
Source: https://playground.shaped.ai
HN: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46779922
A new open-source SQL engine, ShapedQL, targets multi-stage ranking and RAG pipelines by embedding ranking logic directly into queries—promising efficiency but risking opacity in already murky retrieval workflows. The tradeoff: cleaner pipelines or another layer of abstraction to debug.

### 'AI-Built Browser' Claims Collapse Under Scrutiny—Again
Source: https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/26/cursor_opinion/
HN: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46769965
Another demo of an AI agent allegedly assembling a browser from scratch turns out to rely on pre-existing repos and human-curated scaffolding. The pattern repeats: flashy outputs mask brittle dependencies, while the real work remains in debugging the glue code no one shows.
